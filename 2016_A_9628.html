1. 9/20/2016:

First day of class. We covered the syllabus, talked about what public economics is, and started our discussion of pure public goods. We went through the Samuelson condition, Lindahl equilibrium (and why it's not viable), and Bergstrom's rendition of a Bowen (voting) equilibrium for public goods provision. We also discussed Vickrey-Clarke-Groves mechanisms (i.e., second-price auctions) for the provision of public goods.
